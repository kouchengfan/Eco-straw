ECO-STRAW

We use an oxo-biodegradable additive at the manufacturing stage which turns ordinary plastic into a material with a different molecular structure. After a period of useful life, the ECO-STRAW will breakdown in the presence of oxygen. At the end of that process it is no longer a plastic, and has become a material which will disappear in the open environment, the same way as a leaf.

How does it compare to a normal straw?

Same characteristics and strength of a normal drinking straw. Comes in a variety of sizes, regular, jumbo, cocktail and individually wrapped.

How does it break down?

After it’s useful life the oxo-biodegradable additive takes effect and the product begins to break down in the presence of oxygen.

What will it becomes?

The product eventually becomes carbon dioxide, water and biomass without leaving fragments in the environment.

What standards does it meet?

ASTM D6954-04(2013) American standard for exposing and testing plastics that degrade in the enviroment by a combination of oxidisation and biodegradation. This covers:
Tier 1 - Degradability
Tier 2 - Biodegradation
Tier 3 - Eco-toxicity
Regulation (EC) No. 2023/2006 approvals for plastic coming into contact with food.

The life cycle OF our eco-straw

Step ONE

Oxo-biodegradable additive “Reverte(BD93470)” is added at the manufacturing stage and extruded at the factory into our ECO-STRAW.

Step TWO

The product behaves like any other during it’s intended service life. The shelf life of our product (un-opened) maintained at 20℃ is 18 months from the manufactured date.

Step THREE

The useful life (dwell time) of our product is approximately 2-4 months once the product has been opened.

Step Four

After it’s useful life the oxo-biodegradable additive takes effect and the product begins to degrade in the presence of oxygen. After 4 months of opening, the product will begin to show signs of the photo-initiation process, and then a rapid breakdown of properties resulting in acute embrittlement after a further 3-6 months.

Step FIVE

The product will end up in a landfill environment where the biodegradation will take over and eventually the product becomes carbon-dioxide, water and biomass.
